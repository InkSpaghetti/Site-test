Class sorting

Another problem with surveillance is social sorting where a person or group is kept under surveillance because of his or her social status or race within a social society. This sorting happens across the world where in certain cases a young group of minority people are targeted to be under surveillance because of their race, this discrimination is more apparent among societies that allow for racism and bigotry towards ethnic groups of different race, gender and age. Utilitarianism indicates that if surveilling a minority group is for a greater good then it is morally correct but the outrage that the minority group and others linked to it could be more than the greater good. The deontological viewpoint is defined by the universal law that is applied, in this case: groups of minorities are to be treated equally and as such surveillance under discriminatory pretences are illegal. This would stop all surveillance that is targeted at minority groups and it will create equality in the legal system so that no person can be convicted or arrested due to discriminatory reasons or illegal surveillance because of their race, social status or gender. If someone does oppose this law can be considered a bigot and should be shunned from society, I kid of course but if they do oppose it would be for the reasons that they believe is illogical and discriminatory, such as “they are a minority who cares' '. The implementation of the law is a great way to deal with the law unfairly convicting innocent people because of their race or gender through monitoring them and finding ridiculous minor crimes that they use to convict them such as accidentally putting glass in the plastic recycling bin. The second half of deontological reasoning is to treat others as equals and to remove monitoring people under false pretences or discrimination is a form of equality among the minority groups and majority groups. Consequentialism does however conflict with the idea of equality and that the ends justify the means would equate to groups of minority being monitored and having no reparation because of the ends, if they watch a certain group for years, and if just one person happened to steal an apple the operation of surveilling a minority group would be justified so in this instance this system is flawed. Virtue ethics determines that if the character of the minority group is wrong then surveillance is morally justified however if the person who ordered the surveillance on a person or group of minority is corrupt, discriminatory and a bigot then it would be morally wrong.

Safety

Safety is often a common justification for the use of surveillance but it is a reason that many agree with. An example of surveillance being used for safety is the elderly, they often need assistance and when concerned about their safety they are often put under surveillance to make sure they are healthy and alive. From a utilitarianism view point keeping the elderly safe with surveillance is fine because the joy and relief that family members experience is a substantial amount more than the small expense of government funding that is used to keep them safe and healthy. Deontologists agree as creating a universal law of keeping elderly safe and healthy would have little disagreement, it won’t be using people as a means as if they voluntarily agree that a small part of their tax is used to keep nursing homes active and in return the elderly safe. Consequentialism also abides with the idea of keeping the elderly safe and healthy through surveillance is acceptable and that intruding on their privacy is greater than the safety of their lives. Virtue ethics is different as if the government agrees to use a small amount of taxes to surveil the elderly for their safety is doing it out of genuine care for the elderly and not because the public demand it to happen than it it is morally right but if they are doing it to keep the public happy the morality is tainted and is rendered wrong.

With utilitarianism, if exploiting is for the greater good and people it can be viewed as morally right but if the exploiter is doing it for personal gain and is endangering a mass of people it is viewed as morally wrong.
